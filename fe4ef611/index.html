Measurement Bases for Assets

Created2023-12-12|Updated2024-04-28|财务报表分析
|Word Count:368|Reading Time:2mins

Measurement Bases for Assets

  • Historical Cost:

    • The amount originally paid to acquire the asset.
    • Reflects the actual cash outlay made.
    • Is objective and verifiable through documentation.
    • Does not capture changes in asset value over time.

  • Adjusted Historical Cost:

    • Historical cost less accumulated depreciation/amortization.
    • Attempts to allocate historical cost over asset’s useful life.
    • Involves management judgments on useful life.

  • Present Value:

    • Discounted present value of expected future cash flows.
    • Attempts to capture financial worth of expected benefits.
    • Involves subjectivity in projecting cash flows and judging appropriate discount rates.

  • Fair Value:

    • Current market price at which asset could be exchanged.
    • Attempts to capture financial worth of asset under current market conditions.
    • Can involve subjectivity without active markets to use for comparison.

Measurement Bases for Liabilities

  • Historical Cost:

    • The proceeds originally received in exchange for issuing the liability.

  • Present Value:

    • Discounted expected future cash flows required to satisfy the liability.

  • Fair Value:

    • Amount required to transfer the liability in a current exchange.

The appropriate measurement often depends on the nature and use of the asset or liability. Each method has tradeoffs between relevance, reliability, and cost.

Classification of Assets and Liabilities

  • Assets are resources controlled by a company that are expected to provide future economic benefits. They can come from operating activities, investing activities, or financing activities.
  • Liabilities represent obligations that have not yet been paid or revenue that has been received but not yet earned.

Major Asset Categories

  • Current assets - converted to cash within 12 months or one business cycle (e.g. accounts receivable, inventory)
  • Property, plant & equipment - long-term tangible assets used in operations (e.g. buildings, machinery)
  • Investments - financial assets held for income or capital gains (e.g. stocks, bonds)
  • Other assets - intangibles and other long-term assets (e.g. goodwill, deferred tax assets)

Major Liability and Equity Categories

  • Current liabilities - due within 12 months (e.g. accounts payable)
  • Long-term debt - matures after 12 months (e.g. bonds payable)
  • Equity - residual interest belonging to shareholders (e.g. common stock, retained earnings)

Key Measurement Bases

  • Historical cost, present value, fair value
