数量分析类

Probability

 Probability is the measure of the likelihood that an event will occur.

一个时间发生的概率。

Probability is quantified as a number between 0 and 1.

 Independent Event： Two variables are statistically independent if the product of their

marginal distribution is equal to their joint probability.

联合概率分布等于边际概率分布乘积。

边际概率指各自发生的概率。

 Conditional Probability： The conditional probability is the probability of an outcome

given/conditional on another outcome.

Mean/Expected Value

In probability and statistics, mean and expected value are used synonymously to refer to one measure of the central tendency either of a probability distribution or of the random variable characterized by that distribution.

Variance and Volatility

Variance is the expectation of the squared deviation of a random variable from its mean, and it informally measures how far a set of numbers are spread out from their mean.

Variance 方差

Volatility 标准差

The variance is the square of the standard deviation/volatility

Covariance and Correlation

Covariance is a measure of the joint variability of two random variables.

Covariance:协方差，$E(X-\bar X)(Y-\bar Y)$

Correlation：相关性

Correlation refers to the extent to which two variables have a linear relationship with each other.

The most familiar measure of dependence between two quantities is the Pearson’s correlation coefficient, commonly called “correlation coefficient”. The correlation coefficient translates covariance into a unit less metric that runs from -1 to +1. It is the covariance between variables divided by the product of their respective standard deviation.

Pearson’s correlation coefficient：皮尔逊相关系数

less metric：去量纲

Probability Distribution

 We characterize a random variable with a probability distribution.

 It is a mathematical function that can be thought of as providing the probability of occurrence of different possible outcomes in an experiment.

Normal Distribution

 A very common continuous probability distribution.

 The simplest case of a normal distribution is known as the standard normal distribution. It is a special case when mean = 0 and volatility =1.

Hypothesis Testing

 A method of statistical inference.

 Hypothesis tests are used in determining what outcomes of a study would lead to a rejection of the null hypothesis for a pre-specified level of significance.

General hypothesis testing framework:

 Define and interpret the null hypothesis and the alternative.

null hypothesis :原假设，H0

alternative：备择假设，H1

 Distinguish between one-sided and two-sided hypotheses.

one-sided：单尾的假设检验

two-sided：双尾的假设检验

 Describe the confidence interval approach to hypothesis testing.

confidence interval approach:置信区间

 Describe the test of significance approach to hypothesis testing.

test of significance:显著性检验

 Define, calculate and interpret type I and type II errors.

一类错误：当原假设为真时，拒绝了原假设——拒真

二类错误：当原假设为假时，接受了原假设——存伪

 Define and interpret the p-value.

p-value：能够拒绝原假设的最小显著性水平。

Regression

 Linear regression is an approach for modeling the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more explanatory variables.

explanatory variables:自变量

dependent variable：因变量

 In a linear regression with a single independent variable, there are two important coefficients:

 Slope coefficient: a measure of the average change in the dependent given a change in the independent variable.

斜率项。

 Intercept: the predicted value of the dependent if the independent variable is equal to zero

截距项。

Monte Carlo Simulation

 Simulation studies are used to investigate the properties and behavior of various statistics of interest. The technique is used in econometrics when the properties of a particular estimation method are not known.

 Two key stages:

Specification of the data generating model.

Estimation of the parameter of interest in the study

parameter:参数

Time Value of Money

 The principle of the time value of money explains why interest is paid or earned: Interest, whether it is on a bank deposit or debt, compensates the depositor or lender for the time value of money.

 Present Value: the current worth of a future sum of money or stream of cash flows, given a specified rate of return.

 Future Value: The value of an asset or cash at a specified date in the future, based on the value of that asset in the present.

John is forecasting a stock’s performance in 2010 conditional on the state of the economy of the country in which the firm is based. He divides the economy’s performance into three categories of “GOOD”, “NEUTRAL” and “POOR” and the stock’s performance into three categories of “increase”, “constant” and “decrease”.

 Probability that the state of the economy is GOOD is 20%. If the state of the economy is GOOD, the probability that the stock price increases is 80% and the probability that the stock price decreases is 10%

 The probability that the state of the economy is NEUTRAL is 30%. If the state of the economy is NEUTRAL, the probability that the stock price increases is 50% and the probability that the stock price decreases is 30%

 If the state of the economy is POOR, the probability that the stock price increases is 15% and the probability that the stock price decreases is 70%.

Billy, his supervisor, asks him to estimate the probability that the state of the economy is NEUTRAL given that the stock performance is constant. John’s best assessment of that probability is closest to

A. 15.5%

B. 19.6%

C. 20.0%

D. 38.7%

金融市场类

Commercial Bank

 A financial institution that accepts deposits from the public and creates credit.

deposits：存款

creates credit：借款

 Due to their importance in the financial stability of a country, banks are highly regulated in most countries. Banks are generally subject to minimum capital requirements based on an international set of capital standards, known as the Basel Accords.

minimum capital requirements：最低资本要求

Basel Accords:巴塞尔协议

Investment Bank

 A private company that provides various financial-related and other services to individuals, corporations, and governments such as raising financial capital by underwriting or acting as the client’s agent in the issuance of securities.

underwriting：承销

Insurance Companies

 Insurance is usually classified as life insurance 【寿险】and nonlife insurance, with health insurance often being considered to be a separate category. Nonlife insurance is also referred to as property-casualty insurance【 财险】.

 A life insurance contract typically lasts a long time and provides payments to the policyholder’s beneficiaries that depend on when the policyholder【被保人】 dies.

policy：保单

 A property-casualty insurance contract typically lasts one year (although it may be renewed) and provides compensation for losses from accidents, fire, theft, and so on.

 A pension plan【养老保险】 is a form of insurance arranged by a company for its employees. It is designed to provide the employees with income for the rest of their lives once they have retired.

defined benefit plan：固定收益计划,退休后每年收到的资金一定。

defined contribution plan:固定供款计划，退休金根据投资收益情况变动。

Mutual Fund

 A professionally managed investment fund that pools money from many investors to purchase securities.

针对中小投资者，没有严格的门槛。

监管比hedge fund严格。

Hedge Fund

 An investment fund that pools capital from accredited individuals or institutional investors and invests in a variety of assets, often with complex portfolio-construction and risk management techniques.

 Hedge funds are subject to much less regulation than mutual funds because they accept funds only from financially sophisticated individuals and organizations.

主要针对接受得到授权的个人和机构投资者。

ETF Fund

交易所交易基金，能够在交易所交易和赎回。资质要求在hedge fund和mutual fund之间。

Arbitrageur

 Arbitrageur【套利者】: Use derivatives【衍生品】 to take offsetting positions【交易头寸】 in two or more instruments to lock in a profit.

Hedger

 Hedger【风险对冲者】: Use derivatives markets to offset the risk of prices moving unfavorably for their ongoing business activities.

Speculator

 Speculator【投机者】: Use derivatives to seek profits by betting on the future direction of market prices of the underlying asset.

投机增加了市场的流动性。

Broker

 Broker【经纪商】: an individual person who arranges transactions between a buyer and a seller for a commission【佣金】 when the deal is executed.

交易所是会员制，普通投资者不能直接参与交易，需要经纪商代理投资者进行交易。

Market Maker

 Market Maker【做市商】: Maintains bid【买价】 and offer prices【卖价，ask price】 in a given security and stands ready to buy or sell lots of said security, at publicly quoted prices.

Central Counterparty

CCP：中央清算机构。

 A financial institution that provides clearing【清算】 and settlement【结算】 services for trades in foreign exchange, securities, options and derivative contracts.

clearing house：清算所。

 Acts as an intermediary in futures transactions

 Guarantees performance of parties

 Members must post funds with exchange

 Main task is to keep track of transactions, calculate net position of each member daily

margin:保证金

Interest Rate

Treasury Rates【国债利率】

 The rates an investor earns on Treasury bills【短期美国国债】 and Treasury bonds【长期美国国债】.

 Treasury rates are risk-free rates【无风险利率】 in the sense that an investor who buys a Treasury bill or Treasury bond is certain that interest and principal【本金】 payments will be made as promised.

LIBOR【伦敦银行间同业拆借利率】

 A LIBOR quote by a particular bank is the rate of interest at which the bank is prepared to make a large wholesale deposit with other banks.

SOFR

 There are plans to begin phasing out Libor and replace it with a rate based on actual transactions。U.S. has proposed the use of the repo based【回购的】 Secured Overnight【隔夜】 Financing Rate (SOFR)

金融产品类

moneyness:价值状态。

plain vanilla:香草期权。

Bond

Basic Terms

 Face Value: usually represents the principal【本金】 or redemption value【赎回价值】.

 Coupon Rate【息票率】: A coupon payment on a bond is a periodic interest payment that the bondholder receives during the time between when the bond is issued and when it matures.

Coupon【利息】 are normally described in terms of the coupon rate, which is calculated by adding the sum of coupons paid per year and dividing it by the bond’s face value.

 Yield to Maturity【到期收益率】: the internal rate of return earned by an investor who buys the bond today at the market price, assuming that the bond will be held until maturity, and that all coupon and principal payments will be made on schedule.

净现值等于0时对应的收益率。

market risk 主要就是 interest risk

Compounding【复利】

includes interest earned on the interest.

annually /semi-annually 年利/半年利

continious 连续复利

Price Terms

 Accrued Interest【应计利息】: the interest on a bond that has accumulated since the principal investment, or since the previous coupon payment.

从上一个付息日到交易日所累积的利息。

 Clean Price【净价】: The price of a coupon bond【付息债券】 not including any accrued interest. Immediately following each coupon payment, the clean price will equal the dirty price.

 Dirty Price【全价】: A bond pricing quote referring to the price of a coupon bond that includes the present value of all future cash flows, including interest accruing on the next coupon payment

债券市场报价采用净价报价；

全价是不连续的。

Treasury

zero-coupon :零息债

fixed-coupon :固定息债

floating-coupon ：浮息债

 Treasury Bills: a short-term debt obligation backed by the U.S. government with a maturity of one year or less.【一般是零息债】

 Treasury Notes: Mature in 2~10 years; 【一般是固息债】

Treasury Bonds: Mature in more than 10 years. Both make interest payment semi-annually.【一般是固息债】

债券风险：market price【由利率引起】，credit price【履约风险】，liquidity risk

 Duration:

Weighted average of the times until those fixed cash flows are received. 【Macaulay Duration】

Duration also measures the price sensitivity to yield, the rate of change of price with respect to yield.

 Convexity【凸性】: a measure of the non-linear relationship of bond prices to changes in interest rates.

Forward and Futures

Forward【远期合约】

 Agreement to buy/sell asset at future time for certain price.

 Traded in the over-the-counter (OTC) 【场外市场】market.

定制性强，流动性弱。一般到期交割dilivery

Futures【期货】

 Like forward, agreement to buy/sell asset at certain price & time.

 Standardized【标准化的】 and trades on an exchange【交易所】.

 Require a daily settlement【逐日结算】 of gains and losses.

期货最后会delivery【交割】或close-out 【平仓】

Basic Terms

 Long【多头】: means the holder of the position is obliged to buy the underlying instrument at the contract price at expiry. The holder of the position will profit if the price of the underlying instrument goes up, as the price at the contract was lower.

多头有义务买入。

 Short: means having the legal obligation to deliver something at the expiration of the contract, although the holder of the short position may alternately buy back the contract【买回合约，即平仓】 prior to expiration instead of making delivery. Short futures transactions are often used by producers of a commodity to fix the future price of goods they have not yet produced.

Exchange Market

 A derivatives exchange is a central marketplace where participants can trade standardized contracts, including futures and option contracts.

CME:芝商所

 The over-the-counter (OTC) market is an alternative to exchanges where counterparties 【交易对手】can engage in trades directly with each other.

 Margin【保证金】 is collateral【抵押品】 that the holder of a financial instrument has to deposit with a counterparty (most often their broker or an exchange) to cover some or all of the credit risk the holder poses for the counterparty. When the balance in the margin account【保证金账户】 falls below the maintenance margin【维持保证金】, broker executes a margin call【要求追加保证金的电话】. The next day, the investor needs to “top up” the margin account back to the initial margin【初始保证金】 level.

variation margin :变动/补充保证金

Price Terms

 Forward price【约定价格】 is the agreed upon price of an asset in a forward/futures contract.

 If the forward price is higher than the spot price【现货价格】 (or the distant forward price is higher than the near forward price) the Futures curve is said to be normal, or in Contango【期货溢价】.

苹果现货价格=3，期货价格=4，则期货溢价

 If the forward price is less than the spot price (or the distant forward price is less than the near forward price), the Futures curve is said to be inverted, or in Backwardation【现货溢价】.

Forward Rate Agreement

 A FRA is an agreement that a certain rate will apply to a certain principal during a certain future time period.

underlying：标的资产

 The buyer of an FRA locks in a borrowing rate【借款利率】, and the seller locks in a lending rate【借出利率/投资收益】.

Eurodollar Futures【欧洲美元期货】

属于 interest rate futures 利率期货

 Eurodollar is a dollar deposited in a US or foreign bank outside US.

 The most popular interest rate futures contract in the United States is the three-month Eurodollar futures contract traded by the CME Group

Treasury Bond Futures

 In Treasury bonds futures contract, any government bond that has more than 15 years to maturity on the first day of the delivery month and is not callable【可赎回的】 within 15 years from that day can be delivered.

长期国债流动性很差，如果实物交割可能没有可供交割的债券。

 Since the deliverable bonds have very different market values, the Chicago Board of Trade (CBOT) has created conversion factors【转换因子】.

 Cheapest to deliver (CTD)【最便宜可交割债券】 is the cheapest security that can be delivered to the long position to satisfy the contract specifications【合约条款】 and is relevant only for contracts that allow a variety of slightly different securities to be delivered. This is common in Treasury bond futures contracts.

Swaps

Swaps

 A derivative in which two parties agree to exchange one stream of cash flows against another.

Interest Rate Swap【利率互换】

 An interest rate swap (IRS) is a popular and highly liquid financial derivative instrument in which two parties agree to exchange interest rate cash flows, based on a specified notional amount 【特定名义金额】from a fixed rate to a floating rate (or vice versa).

Currency Swap【货币互换】

 A currency swap entails the exchange of principal and interest in one currency for the principal and interest in another currency.

Options

Basic Terms

An option to buy an asset at a particular price is termed a call option.【看涨期权】

An option to sell an asset at a particular price is termed a put option【看跌期权】

The price specified in contract is known as strike price/exercise price【行权价K】.

 Premium【期权费】: amount the option buyer pays and the seller receives for granting the specified rights for the specified period under the option.

Moneyness【价值状态】

 In the money【实值】: Immediate exercise would generate a positive payoff【收益】.

 At the money【平价】: Immediate exercise would generate no payoff.

 Out of the money【虚值】: Immediate exercise would result in a loss.

profit指的是利润，payoff -成本=profit

Value Terms

期权的价值成分。

 Intrinsic Value: The amount that it is in the money, and zero otherwise.

intinsic value : max(S_T-K,0)

 Time Value: The difference between the price of an option (called its premium) and its intrinsic value is due to its time value.

分类

 European Option: can only be exercised at expiration.

 American Option: allow the owner to exercise the option at any time before or at expiration.

 Plain Vanilla Option

Plain vanilla is an adjective describing the simplest version of something, without any optional extras, basic or ordinary. Certain financial instruments like put or call, are often described as plain vanilla options.

可以是欧式期权或标准的美式期权。

 Exotic Option

The opposite of plain vanilla options are exotic options. It is an option which has features making it more complex than commonly traded vanilla options.

如barrier option。

期权定价

Binomial Trees

Binomial Trees【二叉树】: a generalizable numerical method for the valuation of options. A graphical representation of possible intrinsic values that an option may take at different nodes or time periods. The value of the option depends on the underlying stock or bond, and the value of the option at any node depends on the probability that the price of the underlying asset will either decrease or increase at any given node.

适用于美式或欧式。

BSM

 Black-Scholes-Merton (BSM) Model: gives a theoretical estimate of the price of European-style options.

Greek Letters

 Greek Letters: measure a different dimension to the risk in an option position and the aim of a trader is to manage the Greeks so that all risks are acceptable.

delta：股票价格对期权价值的线性影响

gamma：股票价格对期权价值的非线性影响

vega：股票波动率对期权价格的影响

Securitization

Securitization

 The process of creating securities by pooling together various cash-flow producing financial assets such as mortgage loans. These securities are then sold to investors.

Mortgage-Backed Security

 MBS is a type of asset-backed security that is secured by a mortgage or collection of mortgages.

 A mortgage loan【美国市场上的住房抵押贷款】, also referred to as a mortgage, is used by purchasers of real property to raise fund to buy real estate.

风险管理类

Systematic Risk and Unsystematic Risk

Systematic Risk

 Also known as “undiversifiable risk”.

不可分散风险。

 Systematic risk affects the overall market, not just a particular stock or industry.

 Beta is a measure of systematic risk of a security or a portfolio in comparison to the market as a whole. Beta is used in the capital asset pricing model (CAPM) which calculates the expected return of an asset based on its beta and expected market returns.

Unsystematic Risk

 Also known as “diversifiable risk”.

 Company- or industry-specific hazard that is inherent in each investment.

Market Risk

 Risk arising from changes in market risk factors. Include interest rate risk, equity price risk, foreign exchange risk and commodity price risk【商品价格风险】.

Operational Risk

 Refers to financial loss resulting from a host of potential operational breakdowns that we can think in terms of risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes【失败的内部流程】, people, and systems, or from external events【外部事件】.

Credit Risk

 The risk of an economic loss from the failure of a counterparty to fulfill its contractual obligations, or from the increased risk of default during the term of the transaction.

主要分为lending risk和counterpart risk

 Probability of Default【违约概率PD】: describe the likelihood of a default over a particular time horizon. It provides an estimate of the likelihood that a borrower will be unable to meet its debt obligations.

 Loss given Default【违约损失，简记LGD】: is the share of an asset that is lost if a borrower defaults.

RR收回率，LR损失率

 Exposure at Default: can be defined as the gross exposure under a facility upon default of an obligor.

Liquidity Risk

 Funding Liquidity Risk【融资流动性风险】

Relates to a firm’s ability to raise the necessary cash to roll over【展期】 its debt;to meet the cash, margin, and collateral requirements of counterparties; and to satisfy capital withdrawals.

 Trading Liquidity Risk【交易流动性风险】

Is the risk that an institution will not be able to execute a transaction at the prevailing market price【市场现行价格】 because there is no appetite for the deal on the other side of the market. If the transaction cannot be postponed, its execution may lead to a substantial loss.

Expected Loss【预期损失EL】

 Considers how much an entity expects to lose in the normal course of business. Predictable and can be priced into the cost of the goods or by charging commissions.

Unexpected Loss【非预期损失UL】

 Unexpected Loss: Considers how much an entity could lose outside of the normal course of business.

Value at Risk【在险价值】

 A measure of the risk of investments.

 Measures the potential loss in value of a risky asset or portfolio over a defined period for a given confidence interval.

Risk Management Process【风险管理流程】

The name given to a logical and systematic method of identifying【识别】,assessing【测量】, treating【处理】 and monitoring【监控】 the risks involved in any activity or process.

Risk Identification

 Defining types of risks

 Development of risk profiles【风险框架】

Risk Assessment

 How likely is the risk event to happen?【frequency】

 What would be the impact, cost or consequences of that event occurring?【severity】

Risk Treatment

 Develop and implement a plan with specific counter-measures to address the identified risks.

 Document your risk management plan and describe the reasons behind selecting the risk and for the treatment chosen.

avoidance:规避

retention：留存

reduction：分散

transform ：转移【通过保险或者对冲】

Risk Monitoring

 Risk management policies and decisions must be regularly reviewed.

 Risk Managers must monitor activities and processes to determine the accuracy of planning assumptions and the effectiveness of the measures taken to treat the risk.

 Methods can include data evaluation, audit, compliance measurement.

Hedging【对冲】

 An investment position intended to offset potential losses or gains that may be incurred by a companion investment.

 Minimum Variance Hedge Ratio【最小方差对冲比率】: the ratio that minimizes the variance of the hedged position【已经对冲的组合】.

 Basis Risk【基差风险】: is the risk associated with imperfect hedging. It is the financial risk that offsetting investments in a hedge strategy will not experience price changes in entirely opposite directions from each other.

不完美的对冲带来基差风险。

Enterprise Risk Management【全面风险管理】

 An ERM function would be responsible for establishing firm-wide policies and standards, co-ordinate risk management activities across business units and functions, and provide overall risk monitoring for senior management and the board.

Risk Appetite 【风险偏好】

 Is a method to help guide an organization’s approach to risk and risk management. The level of risk that an organization is prepared to accept in pursuit of its objectives.

Stress Test 【压力测试】

 An analysis or simulation designed to determine the ability of a given financial instrument or institution to deal with an economic crisis.

Capital

Capital 分为股权资本和债务资本。覆盖损失一般只用股权资本，破产除外。

While firms set aside reserves in preparation for expected losses, capital is needed to provide a cushion against unexpected loss.

Economic Capital【经济资本】

EC is a VaR-type measure that represents the amount of capital a firm allocates to self-insurance.

Regulatory Capital

The amount of capital a bank or other financial institution has to hold as required by its financial regulator

要求留存的最低资本。
