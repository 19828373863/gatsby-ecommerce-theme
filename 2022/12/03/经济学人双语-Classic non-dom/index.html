Classic non-dom经典的非居民身份


Why Britain should scrap non-dom tax status为什么英国应该取消非居民税收身份


Rishi Sunak, the finance minister, should stop moaning and start reforming.英国财政部长里希苏纳克应该停止抱怨并开始改革


Only a few weeks ago Rishi Sunak, Britain’s chancellor, was seen as the most likely successor to Boris Johnson. Dapper where the prime minister is dishevelled, Mr Sunak was popular for his handling of the pandemic; his personal wealth was seen as a story of can-do competence. His star is now fading fast. A cost-of-living crisis is battering both household finances and his approval ratings: they have plummeted from a peak of +49 during the pandemic to -29 now. And his affluence has abruptly become a source of weakness.就在几周前，英国首相里希·苏纳克被认为是鲍里斯·约翰逊最有可能的继任者。总理头发凌乱而他衣着整齐，苏纳克先生因对新冠大流行的有力处理而广受欢迎；他的个人财富被视为一个励志故事。他的光环正在消失。一场生活成本危机正在打击家庭财务和他的支持率：他们已经从大流行期间的 +49 的峰值暴跌到现在的 -29。他的富裕突然变成了最大的弱点。


On April 6th, the very same day that the Treasury increased payroll taxes, it emerged that his wife, Akshata Murty, has non-domiciled (“non-dom”) tax status. As a non-dom, and in exchange for an annual fee of £30,000 ($39,100), she does not have to pay tax in Britain on income generated abroad. That matters because her foreign assets include a stake in Infosys, a giant Indian IT-services company founded by her father, which generated dividends worth a reported £11.6m for her last year. If Ms Murty had been registered as a UK resident, 38.1% of those earnings would have been due to the government department her husband runs. As it is, zilch.4 月 6 日，也就是财政部提高工资税的同一天，他的妻子 Akshata Murty 出现了无住所（“non-dom”）的纳税身份。作为一个非居民，并以 30,000 英镑（39,100 美元）的年费换取，她不必为在英国产生的海外收入纳税。这一点非常关键，因为她的海外资产包括印孚瑟斯的股份，印孚瑟斯是她父亲创立的一家巨大的印度 IT 服务公司，据报道该公司去年为她带来了价值 1160 万英镑的红利。如果 Murty 女士注册为英国居民，那么这些收入的 38.1% 将纳入她丈夫管理的政府部门。事实上，一分也没有。


Mr Sunak is aggrieved by the focus on Ms Murty’s finances. Wrongly so. He alleges a smear campaign, but it is an odd sort of smear when the principal facts are not in dispute. Mr Sunak’s argument that his wife’s income is her business would make more sense if the government did not treat marriage as a financial merger, in which assets can be transferred between spouses tax-free. To imply that it is sexist to think differently would hold up better if non-dom status were not so archaic: the exemption itself is a legacy of empire and even now a person’s “domicile of origin” is still largely determined by paternal lineage. To say that Ms Murty has done all that Britain asks of her in terms of her tax affairs is deeply disingenuous when he is the person in charge of what is being asked. Sunak 先生对 Murty 女士的财务引发的关注感到冤枉。大错特错。他声称是一场诽谤运动，但当主要事实没有争议时，这就是一种奇怪的诽谤。如果政府不将婚姻视为金融整体，资产就可以在配偶之间免税转移，苏纳克先生认为妻子的收入是她的事就变得合乎情理。如果非居籍身份不是那么过时，表面以不同方式思考是性别歧视更站得住脚：豁免本身就是帝国的遗产，即使现在一个人的“原籍居所”仍然在很大程度上取决于父系血统。Sunak作为税务部门负责人，说 Murty 女士在她的税务事务方面已经完成了英国对她的所有要求，这是非常不坦率的。


On April 8th Ms Murty volunteered to pay British income tax on her global income, without giving up her non-dom status. But the right thing for the chancellor to do is to scrap non-dom status altogether. There is no natural law of taxation which requires it. The non-dom category layers further complexity onto an already-intricate system. The government’s guidance note on how to determine where someone is domiciled features four baffling flow charts and lots of advice to get advice.4 月 8 日，Murty 女士自愿为她的全球收入缴纳英国所得税，但并未放弃她的非居民身份。但对总理来说，正确的做法是完全取消非居民身份。但是没有自然的税收法要求这样做。非居民税收类别在已经很复杂的税收系统上进一步增加了复杂性。政府关于如何确定某人居住地的指导说明包含四个令人费解的流程图和许多获取建议的建议。


The fear that getting rid of non-dom status would discourage talented foreigners from coming to or staying in Britain is not to be dismissed: at the margins, it will deter some people. But the country’s attractions (or more specifically the south-east’s, where the country’s 76,000 non-doms tend to cluster) are not restricted to tax breaks: schools, culture and property rights are all in the mix. And once put down, roots persist. New analysis by academics at the University of Warwick and the London School of Economics suggests that people who have ceased to claim non-dom status do not scarper afterwards.摆脱非居民身份会阻碍有才华的外国人来英国或留在英国的担忧是不容忽视的：在这一方面，它会阻止一些人。但该国的吸引力（或者更具体地说是东南部，该国 76,000 名非本地居民往往聚集在那里）不仅限于税收减免：学校、文化和财产权都在其中。并且一旦施行，便是永恒。华威大学和伦敦经济学院的学者进行的新分析表明，取消非居民身份的人事后不会因此离开英国。


Above all—and especially at a time when living standards in Britain are about to record their steepest drop since records began in the 1950s—it is essential that taxpayers regard taxes as fair. In truth, the tax system is progressive: average tax rates rise with income. But non-dom status is an anomaly; it is overwhelmingly used by the mobile rich. In 2018, 0.3% of those earning less than £100,000 had claimed the exemption; that figure jumps to 41% for those earning £5m and above (among migrants, it zooms to 84%). And since non-doms do not have to tell British authorities where they pay tax on their foreign assets, overseas income can be channelled to tax havens instead. There is no suggestion or evidence that Ms Murty has done anything illegal. But her husband should stop complaining and get rid of the exemption.最重要的是——尤其是在英国的生活水平即将创下自 1950 年代有记录以来的最大跌幅之际——纳税人必须将税收视为公平的。事实上，税收制度是累进的：平均税率随着收入的增加而上升。但是非居民身份状态是反常的；它被那部分流动富人充分利用。 2018 年，收入低于 100,000 英镑的人中有 0.3% 申请了豁免；对于年收入 500 万英镑及以上的人来说，这一数字跃升至 41%（在移民中，这一比例飙升至 84%）。而且由于非居民不必告诉英国当局他们在哪里为他们的外国资产纳税，海外收入可以被引导到避税天堂。没有任何迹象或证据表明 Murty 女士做了任何违法的事情。但她的丈夫应该停止抱怨并取消税务豁免。
