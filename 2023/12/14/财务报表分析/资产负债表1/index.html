Structure and Components of the Balance Sheet

Asset

由企业控制的，由过去的交易或事件产生的，预期能够企业带来收益的资源。

  • resources controlled by the enterprise as a result of past events and from which future economic benefits are expected to flow to the enterprise. (past and future).

  • purchase (investing activities), or generated through business activities (operating activities), or financing activities, such as issuance of debt (all business activities and affect)

Example of Asset

Operative liquid asset【经营性流动资产】

Refers to the current assets (excluding monetary capital) related to the normal production and operation of an enterprise, usually including accounts receivable【应收账款】, prepayments【预付款】, inventories 【存款】and other accounts

Productive long-term assets【生产型长期资产】

It refers to the long-term assets related to the normal production and operation of an enterprise, usually including fixed assets【固定资产】, construction in progress【在建工程】, intangible assets【无形资产】, development expenditure【开发支出】 and long-term deferred expenses【长期待摊费用】.

Long term deferred expenses refer to various expenses that have been paid by the enterprise but have an amortization period of more than 1 year (excluding 1 year), including fixed asset repair expenses【固定资产维修费用】, improvement expenses of leased fixed assets 【租赁固定资产的改造费用】and other deferred expenses with an amortization period of more than 1 year

Other non-core asset【其他非核心资产】

It refers to assets unrelated to the main business of the enterprise, usually investment assets, usually including trading financial assets【交易性金融资产】, available for sale financial assets【可供出售金融资产】, held to maturity investment, long-term equity investment【长期股权投资】 and investment real estate.

Other asset

It refers to the remaining asset accounts that are not divided into operating current assets, productive long-term assets and non core assets, usually including monetary capital【货币资本】, long-term receivables【长期应收款】, deferred income tax assets, other non current assets【其它非流动资产】, etc

Liability

Liabilities (and equity capital) represent the ways in which the funds were raised to acquire the assets.

  • Amounts received but which have not been reported as revenues or income on an income statement【损益表】 and/or will have to be repaid (e.g., unearned revenues【预收账款】). (欠货，欠服务）

Revenue指经营活动带来的收入，然而，INCOME指的是大范围的收入，公式表示Income = Revenue +Gain (除经营活动以外的收入）

  • Amounts that have been reported as expenses on an income statement but have not been paid (e.g., accounts payable【应付账款】, accruals【应计项目】, and taxes payable【应交税费】).（欠钱）

Operating current liabilities【经营性流动负债】

It refers to the current liabilities (excluding financing liabilities) generated by the enterprise in the normal production and operation process, usually including notes payable, accounts payable, advances received, employee salaries payable, taxes payable and other current liabilities【应付票据、应付账款、预收款项、应付员工薪酬、应交税费和其他流动负债】

Financial liability

It refers to the liabilities formed by the financing of an enterprise for the purpose of raising capital. The creditor’s investors aim to obtain capital appreciation. It usually includes short-term loans, long-term loans, bonds payable and other subjects

Other liabilities

It usually includes dividends payable【应付股利】, special accounts payable (专项应付款), deferred income tax liabilities【递延所得税】, other non current liabilities, etc.

Operating working capital (owe) 【经营营运资本】= operating current assets - operating current liabilities.

Equity

Equity = Assets – Liabilities

Equity is increased by contributions by the owners or by profits (including gains) made during the year and is decreased by losses or withdrawals in the form of dividends.

Last resort to the creditors, as shareholders can absorb the losses in liquidation【清算】. For example, some large shareholders.

Pecking order theory：A firm cannot borrow to an extent of full debt and no equity. Distress risk is too high and prevent any creditors.

Format of the Balance Sheet

Classified balance sheet

If a company were to have many assets and liabilities, the balance sheet might become quite difficult to read. Grouping together the various classes of assets and liabilities, therefore, results in a balance sheet format described as a **classified balance sheet.**【因此，将各类资产和负债进行分类汇总，形成的资产负债表格式被称为”分类资产负债表”。】

Current and Noncurrent Assets 【动产和不动产】

Current assets:

Assets expected to be liquidated【变现】 or used up within one year or one operating cycle of the business【经营周期】, whichever is greater.

**Business cycle (**A company ’ s operating cycle is the amount of time that elapses between spending cash for inventory and supplies and collecting the cash from its sales to customers.)

  • Fast Moving Consumer Goods (FMCG)【快速消费品】, like Procter & Gamble 【保洁公司】

  • Long-term cycle【长周期】: infrastructure construction PPP project.

Liquidity - Based Presentation【根据流动性进行排序】

Current/noncurrent format of presentation for the balance sheet.

Identify Current assets

how to identify through time

For most listed companies, such as commercial enterprises, mass-produced industrial enterprises and service industries, the business cycle is usually less than one year.

However, there are still listed companies in some industries, whose business cycle is usually longer than one year, such as shipbuilding enterprises, engineering construction enterprises, etc.

The division of current assets and other non current assets is generally based on whether or not they can be realized or consumed within one year.

However, when the business cycle of a listed company is longer than one year, the current assets and non current assets should be classified according to the business cycle.

how to identify through purpose

some assets can be repeatedly (such as refrigerators, houses, etc.), while some assets may be disposable quickly at one time(such as some daily office supplies, etc.).

Depend on the purpose for which the listed company holds the asset: if the holding purpose is one-year or one-time consumption (expressed or sold, or one-time use completed), it should be a current asset; otherwise, it should be a non current asset.

For example, for a listed company that produces automobiles, if the automobiles it produces are used for sales, the relevant automobiles should be regarded as the current assets of the listed company; if a part of the automobiles it produces is used for its own transportation activities and participates in the operation and turnover of the listed company for a long time, this part of automobiles should be treated as fixed assets

how to identify through prices

for some assets, although the consumption period is more than one year or one business cycle, but the unit price may be low (like stationary), in order to grasp the main contradiction in management, most listed companies regard such assets as current assets management.

Typical Current assets

Working capital (营运资本): the capital of a business which is used in its day-to-day trading operations, calculated as the current assets minus the current liabilities

Monetary capital (货币资金)

  • cash on hand of listed companies

  • deposits of bank settlement accounts

    【银行结算账户（Bank settlement accounts），是指存款人在经办银行开立的办理资金收付结算的人民币活期存款账户。】

  • deposits of foreign banks

  • Draft (汇票)

  • Cheque(本票)

  • Letter of credit deposit (L/C)信用证保证金

  • Letter of guarantee (L/G) 保函保证金

Cash on hand: refers to the cash retained by the enterprise to meet the sporadic payment needs in the business process (such as the cash with thousands of change in the supermarket cashier).

Bank deposits: including various deposits deposited by enterprises in banks or other financial institutions (except for those classified as other monetary funds).

Restricted monetary capital:

  • for operating activities, earnest money, L/C, L/G

  • collateral, which is quite uncertain.

Analysis-Cash and cash equivalent

Too little cash, firm cannot function well; Too much cash, firm may waste investment opportunities.

Whether the firm has reasonable reserve in cash and cash equivalent?

  2. Analyze the freedom of monetary capital

Besides cash and cash equivalent, many monetary capital【货币资本】 have been scheduled to have certain intention, and the firm has less control over them.

货币资本（Monetary Capital）是指公司拥有的以货币形式存在的资产，包括现金、银行存款、短期投资和应收票据等。它们是公司经营活动所持有的流动资金，用于支付日常运营费用、债务偿还和投资等。

货币资金（Monetary Fund），则是指公司现金和现金等价物的总额，包括现金、银行存款和其他货币资金。货币资金是公司可立即使用的现金或能够迅速变现的资金，用于满足即时支付需求。

Analysis- Account Receivable (应收账款)

Account Receivable：The commercial creditor’s rights formed by the business of selling goods, materials and providing services on credit. Such claims shall be collected from customers.

(1) Analyze the Authenticity and Rationality【分析真实性和合理性】

  • Calculate the ratio of account receivable /total asset and compare it with the industry mean (median)【横向比较】

  • Compare the account receivable ratio with the historical record to see whether there is sudden increase.【比较应收账款比率与历史记录，以确定是否存在突然增加。】【纵向比较】

  • If there is sudden increase in the account receivable ratio, need to carefully calculate the growth rate of asset and growth rate of sales.

    • Maybe financial distress of customer【可能是客户出现财务困境】

    • Maybe competiveness of the firm shapely decrease

Analysis- Turnover of account receivable

The borrower composition of account receivable【应收账款的借款人构成；】

calculate and compare the turnover of account receivable and turnover of inventory.【计算并比较应收账款周转率和存货周转率；】

turnover of the account receivable: Total sales/average account receivable (0.5*(begging account receivable + ending account receivable )【应收账款周转率：总销售额除以平均应收账款（0.5 *（期初应收账款 + 期末应收账款））；】

turnover of the inventory: Total sales/average inventory【存货周转率：总销售额除以平均存货。】

It is not the higher the turnover of account receivable , the better. Too higher turnover in receivable will lower the inventory turnover.【”高应收账款周转率并不意味着越好，过高的应收账款周转率会降低存货周转率。】

周转越快，回款越快。但如果企业的竞争力没那麽强，较短的周转期会要求客户尽快回款，导致自身市场竞争力下降，导致企业收入和利润下降，库存增加，库存周转速度下降。

  • If turnover of receivable and inventory both increase, very good.【如果应收账款周转率和存货周转率都增加，这是非常好的。】

  • If turnover of receivable increase while turnover of inventory decrease, means the firm expand production or lower the credit sale【赊售】.【如果应收账款周转率增加，而存货周转率下降，则意味着公司扩大了生产或减少了赊销。】

  • If turnover of receivable decrease and inventory turnover increase: the firm increase credit sale, maybe to bad market expectation.

Inventories-Basic calculation

Include: Raw material (原材料) — Working in Progress (在产品)—finished good(产成品)

Detail: material cost, direct labor and manufacturing overhead

Exclude:

-Abnormal amounts of wasted materials, labor, and overhead.

-Storage costs, unless they are necessary prior to a further production process.( Period cost)

-Administrative overheads. ( Period cost)

-Selling costs. ( Period cost)

When inventory sold, the cost in inventory will be transferred to cost of good sold using

IFRS: FIFO, Weighted average

GAAP: FIFO, LIFO, Weighted average

Depreciation

If the market value (under US GAAP) of a company’s inventory falls below its carrying amount, the company must write down the value of the inventory.

The loss in value is reflected in the income statement. Under IFRS, if inventory that was written down in a previous period subsequently increases in value, the amount of the original write-down is reversed. Subsequent reversal of an inventory write-downis not permitted under US GAAP.

Structure of depreciation, specifically WIP.

What inventory deprecation 【存货减值】means?

1 If the depreciation is proper—Fault of management, improvement in inventory management

2 If the depreciation is not proper: Inventory decline in value, under-depreciated in the early years.

Inventory not decline in value, improve future performance.

4 Calculate the inventory turnover=Operation cost/inventory

l Operation cost include purchasing cost, conversion cost, and other cost that support the firm’s production.

  1. Firm want to reduce inventory and increase the turnover in one accounting period.
  2. There is a balance between account receivable turnover and inventory turnover. Analyst should not think the higher inventory turnover is the better.
  3. There is basically two strategy: one is keep technique advantage and increase gross profit margin, the other is to improve operation efficiency to earn smallgross profits but quick turnover and mass volume. Analyst need to first judge what is the firm’s strategy. For the latter strategy,analyst need to focus more on inventory. 5 Compare account receivable + note receivable and unearned income

Tangible Assets (PPE, property, plant and equipment)

Tangible assets are long - term assets with physical substance that are used in company operations.

These noncurrent assets are carried at their historical cost less any accumulated depreciation【折旧】 or accumulated depletion【折耗】. Historical cost generally consists of vendor invoice cost 【发票成本】, freight (运输) cost, and any other additional costs incurred to make the asset operable.【历史成本通常包括供应商发票成本、运输成本和为使资产达到可使用状态而发生的其他额外成本。】

Include land, buildings, equipment, machinery, furniture, and natural resources owned by the company, such as copper mines, oil and gas ,properties, and timberlands as raw material.

经营性利润： Operation income

投资性利润：Investment income

Operation income and investment income substitute.

Low operation income, especially compared with investment asset income.

Fixed assets (固定资产)

The life of the asset is typically longer than 1 year. Use for operation.

The characteristics:

(1) Long term holding and make important contribution in operation.

(2) They cost large amount of money, operation risk is huge.

(3) The size and structure of the fixed assets reflect the technique advantage of the firm.

(4) The depreciation and depreciation preservation affect the earning to a great extent.

Evaluate the change of the size of fixed assets and operating income?

Evaluate the size of fixed assets and gross profit?

Other analysis

(1) Calculate the turnover of fixed asset= revenue/fixed assets to reflect the efficiency of using certain amount of fixed asset to generate profit.

(2) Analyze whether the fixed asset can preserve its value: the scarcity (real estate vs computer), the industry characteristics (the equipment of steel company)

It is the on-going fixed assets, including new constructions, improvement or expansion, large scale repair.

Firm need to report the project name, the investment amount, the percentage of completion information in the footnote.

Some firms will make the project unfinished for a long time.
