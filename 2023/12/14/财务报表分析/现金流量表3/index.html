1.3现金流量表的分析

Four Steps to Analyze a Cash Flow Statement

Investigate the adequacy of cash flow【现金流的充足性】 generated by business activities

  • core profit yield of cash flow= net cash flow from operating activities / core profit
  • A ratio between 1.2-1.5 is ideal for most companies
  • Example: Guizhou Maotai had a high net profit cash ratio in 2010-2011 as unearned income【预收账款】 from distributors【经销商】 increased significantly. But the ratio dropped in 2012-2013 as dealer enthusiasm and inventory issues affected sales.

核心利润现金流量收益率是一个比率，用于衡量企业活动产生的现金流量的充足程度。它的计算公式为经营活动产生的净现金流量/核心利润。对于大多数公司而言，1.2-1.5之间的比率是理想的。如果比率小于1，则表明公司没有产生足够的现金流来支持其运营。如果比率大于1，则表明公司产生的现金流超过了其支持运营所需的现金流。该比率可用于评估公司的财务健康状况和可持续性。

核心利润现金流量收益率可以被用来衡量企业经营活动的盈利能力与现金流量情况的一致性。这一指标反映了企业的盈利能力是否能够转化为现金流量，并且能够持续为企业提供稳定的现金流。

较高的核心利润现金流量收益率通常意味着企业能够有效地将盈利转化为现金流量，并且具有良好的经营稳定性。相反，较低的核心利润现金流量收益率可能意味着企业存在一些问题，如利润质量不佳【如应收账款较多】、现金流短缺或不稳定等。

Guizhou Maotai (600900. SH) is a leading enterprise in liquor industry in China. It was listed on Shanghai Stock Exchange in 2001. Due to the tight demand of Maotai liquor in the past few years, Guizhou Maotai often adopts【采用】 the way of payment first and then taking goods when selling, so there are more unearned income in advance【贵州茅台经常采用先付款后提货的方式进行销售，因此预收款项较多。】.

From the operating accounts payable 【应付账款】in the following table . In 2010 and 2011, the liquor market in Maotai, Guizhou Province was popular, and the unearned income from distributors increased significantly, and the net profit cash ratio was above 100%.

In 2012, although the net profit still maintained a high growth rate, the operating payable items did not increase significantly, and the inventory increased significantly,【应付账款减少造成现金流入减少；存货增加】 which indicated that the enthusiasm of dealers to pay money decreased and the inventory increased during the sales in Maotai, Guizhou.

Affected by these two projects, the net profit cash ratio of Maotai in Guizhou Province dropped to 85.1%.In 2013, the growth rate of net profit in Maotai, Guizhou decreased significantly, the operating accounts payable decreased significantly, and the inventory continued to rise sharply. Affected by this, the net profit cash ratio of that year fell below 80%.

The changes of accounts payable【应付账款】 and inventory in 2012 and 2013 showed that the sales of Maotai in Guizhou had problems in these two years. In fact, this is mainly affected by the State Council’s 【政府】strict restriction of the “three public” funding policy issued in March 2012, prohibiting the use of public funds to buy high-grade liquor. As a representative of high rank liquor, Maotai in Guizhou is the first to bear the brunt.

Examine the expansion implication【影响】 of cash outflows from investment activities【投资活动的现金流出对扩张的影响】

分析投资活动中现金流出对企业扩张的影响。

  • 直接投资：Large outflows for purchase/construction of fixed assets and intangibles【无形资产】 indicates expansion plans
  • 间接投资：Outflows for financial investments indicates external expansion 【外部扩张】or financial management activities（M&A【兼并&收购】）【财务管理活动】

如果企业的投资活动现金流出主要用于金融投资,这表示企业可能正在通过外部投资来扩张业务,或者正在频繁进行财务管理。

例如,企业可能通过对其他公司的股权投资来实现外部扩张。或者可能正在进行像买卖证券这样的财务管理活动来获取投资收益。

所以如果从投资活动现金流出中看到有大量金融投资支出,这往往意味着企业正在通过外部渠道扩张业务,或者正积极进行财务管理来获得投资收入。

  • Example: LETV’s large investing outflows for assets from 2016-2018, with little financial investment, indicates focus on assets to support growth

  1. If a company has large cash outflows for purchases and construction of fixed assets and intangible assets, it indicates expansion plans. These capital expenditures support growth of business operations.

  2. If a company pays large amounts of cash for financial investments, it may be expanding through external investment channels or frequent financial management.

  3. If cash outflows for capital expenditures are very small, it signals the company likely does not have major expansion plans or limited scope【规模、范围】 of expansion.

  4. Different cash outflow structures reflect how a company is expanding. Internal capital spending indicates growth in operations, while financial investments indicate external growth through acquisitions【收购】 or financial activities.

  5. It is a pity that the increase of assets in support of operating activities has not brought about the real development of business activities.

In summary, analyzing investing cash outflows reveals a company’s expansion intentions, method of expansion (internal vs. external), and capital intensity of expansion. This ties closely to【紧密相关】 the firm’s development strategy and financial situation.

Investigate the scale, structure and direction of cash flows from financing activities【融资活动的现金流量的规模、结构和方向】

  • Look at capital from shareholders, financial institutions, bond issuance

  • See where the money is being spent - operations, assets, acquisitions, debt repayment

  • Example: LETV received lots of investment from shareholders and institutions from 2016-2018, used to supplement operations and purchase assets to fuel “burning money” strategy

According to LETV’s report, the main sources of funds for enterprises are the following:

First, shareholders of listed companies, for example, in 2016, LETV obtained about 11.1 billion yuan of shareholders’ capital by issuing shares. It is not known whether these shareholders who purchased shares issued by LETV in that year made profits or losses;

Second, the non controlling shareholders of subsidiarie【子公司】, also known as minority shareholders, for example, in 2017 and 2018, the minority shareholders of subsidiaries invested 3.311 billion yuan and 730 million yuan in subsidiaries respectively.

Third, various financial institutions. From 2016 to 2018, financial institutions have provided RMB 13 billion to LETV in three years.The funds provided by shareholders and financial institutions are partly used to supplement the cash gap of operating activities, and part of the funds are used to supplement the capital demand for the purchase and construction of fixed assets and intangible assets. It is the shareholders and financial institutions that spare no effort to support LETV, which makes this enterprise have enough confidence to burn money.

In summary:

When we look at the cash flow of financing activities, we mainly look at four aspects.

First, do you see if the shareholders have given money?

Second, do you think various financial institutions give money to enterprises?

Third, is it true that enterprises have issued bonds to raise funds (LETV did not issue bonds between 2016 and 2018)?

Fourth, look at where the money raised by enterprises is spent?

The funds obtained by enterprises are mainly used in five aspects.

First, supplement the operating cash flow, just like LETV;

Second, enterprises should continue to buy fixed assets, such as machinery, equipment, or intangible assets .Assets, such as trademarks and patents, are like LETV;

Third, the acquisition of companies or other investments, such as LETV’s 2016;

Fourth, it’s used to pay off debts. Sometimes, enterprises need to borrow new debts to repay old debts;

Fifth, there was no demand, but we still had to borrow money. This situation should not be the last, it is entirely the enterprise itself. There’s money to play with. The money raised in this situation will increase the scale of monetary funds.

Consider what cash flow structure enables sustainable long-term development

  • Ability to collect sales advances and get payment terms【支付条款】 from suppliers (“two ends eat” ability)【the supplier can collect payment after company a sells the goods】
  • Competitive advantage and good business credit needed

The analysis examines the operating, investing and financing cash flow activities to understand the company’s profitability, expansion plans, funding sources and sustainability.
