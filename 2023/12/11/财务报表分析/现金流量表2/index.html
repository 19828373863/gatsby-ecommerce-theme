1.2现金流量与企业生命周期

发表于2023-12-11|更新于2024-01-02|财务报表分析
|字数总计:3.4k|阅读时长:13分钟

Introduction

  • Discusses the four phases of a firm’s life cycle: introduction, growth, maturity, and decline
  • Each phase has distinct patterns of cash flows from operating, investing, and financing activities

Characteristics of the Phases

Introduction Phase【初创阶段】

“只会要钱、净会花钱”

  • Revenues are low or nonexistent as product is developed
  • Negative cash from operations as product is developed and company incurs losses
  • Negative cash from investing to build infrastructure, facilities etc.
  • Positive cash from financing, likely stock issues or debt, to fund negative cash from operations and investing

Advanced Cell Technology (Introduction Phase)

Background:

  • Founded in 2005 as a biotechnology company focused on developing stem cell treatments【干细胞治疗】
  • Operates in a nascent industry【新兴行业】 - stem cell therapies are still in early stages of research and development
  • Has not yet managed to develop any commercially viable products

Financial Data & Analysis:

  • the negative cash flows from both operating and investing activities, funded by large positive cash flows from financing activities.
  • the large net losses (72.8 million in 2011 and 28.5 million in 2012).
  • Negative cash flow from operations ($14.6M loss) in 2012 demonstrates【说明了】 lack of revenue-generating commercial products
  • Negative cash from investing ($1.9M) as company builds out labs, facilities, and infrastructure needed to develop stem cell technologies
  • Positive cash from financing provided by stock shares issuances to investors willing to finance company’s research operations
  • Consistent and large net losses (almost $30M in 2012) show company is still focused on product development, not commercialization

Growth Phase

  • Revenues increasing quickly as product gains transction
  • Positive and growing cash from operations as company moves towards profitability
  • Very negative cash from investing to support rapid growth
  • Positive cash from financing likely from debt and stock issues to fund growth
  • Increasing profits and cash balances

Black Diamond Group (Growth Phase)

Background:

  • Founded in 2009, rents modular structures (e.g. containers) to oil/gas, construction industries
  • Completed IPO in 2010 to raise expansion capital
  • Buy ratings 【寻求评级】from multiple analysts given high sales/profitability growth

Financial Data & Analysis:

  • Rapid rise in net income from $41M to $47.4M in one year indicates fast market share gains as company scales
  • 50% increase in operating cash flow shows high market demand for rental services driving revenue growth
  • Large jump in investing cash flow (almost 2x) suggests aggressive asset expansion to meet rising demand
  • Issuance of equity shares【权益股票】 provides external financing 【外部融资】to supplement【补充】 capacity investments 【产能投入】

Key Takeaways:

  • Growth phase defined by surging【increasing/飞速增长的】 product demand that requires large investments in productive assets & capacity
  • Cash from operations rises but still insufficient to fully fund growth plans, so company uses financing as well

Maturity Phase

  • Revenues and profits stabilize or grow slowly
  • Consistently positive cash from operations that can fund investing activities
  • Less need for external financing as company generates own cash
  • Excess cash used for dividends, stock buybacks 【股票回购】to return to shareholders【股票投资者】

Cedar Fair L.P 【有限责任公司】(Maturity Phase) Background:

  • Founded 1983, operates amusement and water parks in North America
  • Portfolio 【资产组合】includes famous parks like Cedar Point and Knott’s Berry Farm
  • Mature company with well-established brand name and parks

Financial Data & Analysis:

  • Consistent high net income around $100M per year signifying stability
  • Strong operating cash flows of $285.9M in 2012 demonstrates reliable profits,
  • Able to fund investments of $177.8M and distributions out of own cash flow
  • Decreasing reliance on external financing like debt
  • Growing cash balance from $9.8M to $78.8M

Key Takeaways:

  • Marks maturity phase where company has firmly entrenched position【非常牢固的地位】 in market
  • Substantial 【大额的】and predictable cash generation from operations
  • More focused on returning cash to shareholders than expansion investments
  • Self-funds investments without need for much external financing.

Decline Phase

  • Revenues and profits declining
  • Cash from operations declining substantially【大额地】 or negative
  • Cutback 【削减】in investing activities, possible asset sales【削减投资活动，可能伴随资产出售】
  • Little ability to obtain external financing
  • Cash squeezed【紧张/拮据】, possible bankruptcy

Warner Music Group (Decline Phase) Background:

  • Originally founded in 1929, historically one of the largest music publishing companies
  • Decline of physical music formats like CDs erased main business revenue streams

Financial Data & Analysis:

  • String of growing net losses from -$51M in 2008 to -$145M in 2010
  • Sharp falling cash flows from operations, almost halved from $304M to $150M
  • Minimal financing activity, only reissuing and retiring debt
  • Volatile【波动的】 investing cash flows as business restructures operations【the company disposed of 【处置】certain investments】

Key Takeaways:

  • Collapse of traditional physical music formats sinks revenues and profits
  • Attempts reorganizations【尝试重组】 and asset sales but struggles without cash infusion【资金注入】
  • Few sources willing to provide external financing to distressed【困难重重的】 business【企业】

Free cash flow

https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/HRB/financials/annual/cash-flow

Here are the steps to calculate free cash flow for a company:

  1. Find the net income on the company’s income statement.

  2. Add back any non-cash expenses like depreciation and amortization. This gives you operational cash flow.

  3. Subtract capital expenditures (money spent to buy assets like property, equipment etc.) from the operational cash flow.

This gives you free cash flow.

The formula is:

Free Cash Flow = Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures

Where:

Operating Cash Flow = Net Income + Adjustments for non-cash items

So in simple terms:

Free Cash Flow = Net Income + Depreciation/Amortization - Capital Expenditures

The key things to remember:

  • Free cash flow represents the cash a company generates after accounting for reinvestment in the business
  • It’s a useful metric of how much cash profit a company can distribute to shareholders, pay down debt etc.
  • Higher free cash flow typically signals financial health and strength.

附录

**Net income (loss)**：净利润（亏损）- 公司在一定期间内的总收入减去总费用后所剩下的金额，如果结果为正值则表示净利润，如果为负值则表示净亏损。

Adjustments to reconcile net income (loss) to net cash from operating activities：为了将净利润（亏损）调和到经营活动产生的净现金流量而进行的调整项。

Depreciation and amortization：折旧和摊销费用 - 固定资产和无形资产在其使用寿命内价值的减少。

Non-cash equity based compensation expense：非现金股权激励费用 - 公司向员工授予股权作为激励而产生的费用，但不涉及现金支付。

Loss on early debt extinguishment：提前偿债亏损 - 公司提前偿还债务所产生的损失。

Loss on impairment of goodwill and other intangibles：商誉和其他无形资产减值亏损 - 公司商誉和其他无形资产价值的减少所导致的亏损。

Loss on impairment/retirement of fixed assets, net：固定资产减值/报废亏损，净额 - 公司固定资产减值或报废所导致的亏损，扣除了由于资产出售获得的收益。

Gain on sale of other assets：其他资产出售收益 - 公司出售其他资产所产生的收益。

Net effect of swaps：掉期交易的净效应 - 掉期交易（利率、外汇等）的净影响。

Amortization of debt issuance costs：债务发行成本摊销 - 将债务发行时产生的费用分摊到债务的期间。

Unrealized foreign currency (gain) loss on notes：票据的未实现外币汇兑损益 - 因汇率波动引起的未实现的外币兑换损失或收益。

Other non-cash income：其他非现金收益 - 公司从其他非现金来源获得的收益。

Deferred income taxes：递延所得税 - 由于税务条例规定了不同时期的税收应缴纳的时间差而引起的递延所得税费用或收益。

Excess tax benefit from unit-based compensation expense：基于单位的补偿费用的超额税益 - 基于单位的补偿计划所产生的税收差异。

Change in operating assets and liabilities：营运资产和负债的变动 - 与经营活动相关的资产和负债项目的变化。

(Increase) decrease in current assets：流动资产的增加（减少）- 与经营活动相关的流动资产增加或减少。

(Increase) decrease in other assets：其他资产的增加（减少） - 与经营活动相关的其他资产增加或减少。

Increase (decrease) in accounts payable：应付账款的增加（减少） - 与经营活动相关的应付账款增加或减少。

Increase (decrease) in accrued taxes：应交税费的增加（减少） - 与经营活动相关的应交税费增加或减少。

Increase (decrease) in self-insurance reserves：自保储备金的增加（减少）- 与经营活动相关的自保储备金增加或减少。

(Decrease) increase in deferred revenue and other current liabilities：递延收入和其他流动负债的减少（增加）- 与经营活动相关的递延收入和其他流动负债减少或增加

Net cash provided by (used in) operating activities：经营活动产生的净现金流量 - 公司在一定期间内通过其经营活动获得的现金余额与支出的现金余额之差。

Cash flows from investing activities：投资活动现金流量 - 公司在一定期间内通过其投资活动获得的现金余额与支出的现金余额之差。

Purchase of property, plant and equipment：购买房地产、厂房及设备 - 公司在一定期间内购买房地产、厂房及设备支出的现金余额。

Sale of property, plant and equipment：出售房地产、厂房及设备 - 公司在一定期间内出售房地产、厂房及设备获得的现金余额。

Purchase of intangible assets：购买无形资产 - 公司在一定期间内购买无形资产支出的现金余额。

Acquisition of business：收购企业 - 公司在一定期间内收购其他企业所支出的现金余额。

Other investing activities：其他投资活动 - 公司在一定期间内进行其他类型的投资活动所产生的现金余额。

Net cash provided by (used in) investing activities：投资活动产生的净现金流量 - 公司在一定期间内通过其投资活动获得的现金余额与支出的现金余额之差。

Cash flows from financing activities：筹资活动现金流量 - 公司在一定期间内通过其筹资活动获得的现金余额与支出的现金余额之差。

Proceeds from issuance of debt：债务发行所得 - 公司在一定期间内通过债务发行获得的现金余额。

Repayment of debt：偿还债务 - 公司在一定期间内偿还债务所支出的现金余额。

Repurchase of common stock：回购普通股 - 公司在一定期间内回购普通股所支出的现金余额。

Common stock dividends paid：普通股股息支付 - 公司在一定期间内支付给普通股股东的股息所支出的现金余额。

Other financing activities：其他筹资活动 - 公司在一定期间内进行其他类型的筹资活动所产生的现金余额。

Net cash provided by (used in) financing activities：筹资活动产生的净现金流量 - 公司在一定期间内通过其筹资活动获得的现金余额与支出的现金余额之差。

Effect of exchange rate changes on cash：货币汇率变化对现金的影响 - 公司在一定期间内由于货币汇率变化而导致现金余额发生变化的金额。

Net increase (decrease) in cash and cash equivalents：现金及现金等价物的净增加（减少） - 公司在一定期间内现金及现金等价物余额的变化情况。

Sale of other assets (其他资产销售): 指销售与公司正常业务无关的资产所带来的现金流入。

Capital expenditures (资本支出): 指用于获取或改进长期资产（如房地产、厂房和设备）的现金支出。

Net cash for investing activities (投资活动的净现金流量): 投资活动现金流入与现金流出之间的差额，反映公司通过投资活动所用或产生的净现金流量。

Net (payments) borrowings on revolving credit loans - previous credit agreement (循环信贷贷款净（还款）额 - 以前的信贷协议): 指在以前信贷协议框架下的循环信贷贷款的净借款或净还款金额。

Net (payments) borrowings on revolving credit loans - existing credit agreement (循环信贷贷款净（还款）额 - 现行的信贷协议): 指在现行信贷协议框架下的循环信贷贷款的净借款或净还款金额。

Term debt borrowings (长期债务借款): 指通过发行公司债券或向金融机构贷款等方式，筹集长期债务所带来的现金流入。

Note borrowings (短期债务借款): 指通过发行公司短期票据或向金融机构贷款等方式，筹集短期债务所带来的现金流入。

Derivative settlement (衍生品结算): 指衍生品交易活动所带来的现金流入或现金流出。

Term debt payments, including early termination penalties (长期债务偿还，包括提前终止违约金): 指偿还公司债券或向金融机构还款等方式所带来的现金流出。其中包括提前终止合同所需支付的罚金。

Distributions paid to partners (分配给合伙人的收益): 指支付给公司合伙人的分配收益所带来的现金流出。

Payment of debt issuance costs (偿还债务发行成本): 指偿还公司发行债券或票据等债务所需支付的费用所带来的现金流出。

Exercise of limited partnership unit options (有限合伙单位期权行使): 指公司员工行使有限合伙股权期权所带来的现金流入。

Excess tax benefit from unit-based compensation expense (基于单位股份补偿费用的超额税收收益): 指由于员工股份计划所带来的税收优惠，导致公司实际缴纳的税收低于预计税收所带来的超额税收收益所带来的现金流入。

Net cash for financing activities (筹资活动的净现金流量): 筹资活动现金流入与现金流出之间的差额，反映公司通过筹资活动所用或产生的净现金流量。

EFFECT OF EXCHANGE RATE CHANGES ON CASH AND CASH EQUIVALENTS (汇率变动对现金及现金等价物的影响): 指由于汇率变动所带来的影响，导致公司现金及现金等价物数额发生变化所带来的现金流量影响。

CASH AND CASH EQUIVALENTS (现金及现金等价物): 指公司持有的现金、银行存款和其他短期有期证券，其具有高度流动性且可立即用于支付债务或进行投资的资金。

Net increase (decrease) for the year (年度净增加（减少）): 指在整个财务年度内现金及现金等价物的净增加或净减少金额。

Balance, beginning of year (年初余额): 指财务年度开始时的现金及现金等价物余额。

Balance, end of year (年末余额): 指财务年度结束时的现金及现金等价物余额。
