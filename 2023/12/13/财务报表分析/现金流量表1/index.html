Cash flow statement format

Operating Activities：Cash inflows and outflows from transactions and events that affect operating income

lnvesting Activities：Cash inflows and outflows from loaning money to others, investing in securities, or in assets (e.g., equipment) used to produce goods and services.

Financing Activities：Cash inflows and outflows from borrowing money, selling stock, and paying dividends

直接法

“Accrual income” 是一个会计术语，它指的是尚未实际收到但已经应计入企业财务报表中的收入。

“Non-cash accruals” 是指会计中的一种调整项，用于反映企业在财务报表周期内发生的非现金交易或未实际收到的收入或费用。这些调整项包括将未实现的销售收入、已计提但尚未支付的费用、预收收入等纳入财务报表中，即使这些交易尚未以现金的形式发生。

非现金应计项的例子包括：按照权责发生制确定的销售收入、计提的折旧和摊销费用、预算内的费用、税收预提等。这些项目都是在财务报表周期内根据会计准则和原则进行调整的，以更准确地反映企业在报表期间的经营状况和财务表现。

COMPONENTS AND FORMAT

Concept

Cash

In the monetary capital【货币资本】, what the firm really has freedom to use and withdraw any time.

For example, the current deposit【活期存款】.

Include:

(1) cash on hand,

(2) bank deposit: the deposit that can be used for payment at any time, as well as other monetary funds such as foreign deposits, bank draft deposits【银行汇票存款】, etc.

Not include: fixed deposits that cannot be withdrawn in advance should not be used as cash. Fixed deposits that can be withdrawn by notifying financial enterprises in advance should be included in the scope of cash.

Cash equivalent

There are some differences in the meaning of cash equivalents between accounting standards of different countries.

The definition of “cash equivalents” in the statement of Financial Accounting Standard No. 95 of the United States refers to short term investments with an initial maturity of less than 3 months (including 3 months).

In IAS 7 - cash flow statement, cash equivalents are defined as short-term investments that can be converted into known amounts of cash at any time, with high liquidity and low risk of change in value

Cash flow

Cash flow refers to the amount of cash and cash equivalent inflows and outflows of an enterprise in a certain period of time.

For example, an enterprise obtains cash from selling goods, providing services, selling fixed assets, borrowing from banks, etc. pays cash for purchasing raw materials, receiving services, purchasing PPE (Purchase of Plant ,Property and Equity), investing, paying debts, etc., and forms a cash outflows of an enterprise.

Cash flow information can show whether the business is in good condition, whether the funds are tight and whether the solvency【偿付能力】 of the enterprise is large or small, so as to provide very useful information for investors, creditors【债权人】 and enterprise managers.

It should be noted that the conversion between different forms of corporate monetary capital will not generate cash flow.

For example, the withdrawal of cash from banks by an enterprise is a conversion of cash deposit form of an enterprise, which does not flow out of the enterprise and does not constitute cash flow; similarly, the conversion between cash and cash equivalents does not belong to cash flow.

  • The cash flow statement has subsections relating specific items to the operating, investing, and financing activities of the company. (the invisible hand, business model)【现金流量表的分部分涉及公司的经营、投资和筹资活动与具体项目的相关性。（”看不见的手”，商业模型）】

  • Two presentation formats are available: the direct and the indirect.【有两种可选的现金流量表呈现格式：直接法和间接法。】

Classification of Cash Flows and Noncash Activities

Operating activities

company ’ s day - to - day activities that create revenues, such as selling inventory and providing services

Example: 东阿阿胶—purchasing quite a lot of donkey fur on the financial statement as inventory.

maybe betting for the price increase of the fur. Some other companies may restore inventory in the downturn, to avoid the risk of fluctuation of raw material prices.

Inflow: company ’ s day - to - day activities that create revenues, such as selling inventory and providing services

  1. cash received from the sale of goods and the provision of labor services, including the value-added tax collected from the sales recipient and the purchaser, and accounts collected in the current period, less the cash returned to the sale in the current period. The cash received from the enterprise’s sales of materials and the business of purchasing and selling on a commission basis is also included in the project

  2. tax refund received. Reflect various taxes and fees returned by the enterprise, such as value-added tax, consumption tax, business tax, income tax, additional return of education fee, etc.

  3. other cash received related to operating activities, such as lease fees if the firm is a leasing firm, fines, cash income compensated by individuals in the loss of current assets, donation fees, fines, leasing fees from investment real estate, government subsidy, etc.

Out flows: company ’ s day - to - day activities that create revenues, such as selling inventory and providing services

  1. cash paid for purchasing goods and receiving services. The cash paid for purchasing materials, commodities and services in the current period (including value added tax), as well as the paying of the unpaid items and prepayments in the current period for purchasing materials, commodities and services in the earlier period. The cash received due to purchase return in the current period is deducted from the project.
  1. the cash paid to employees, including the wages, bonuses, various allowances and subsidies actually paid to the employees in the current period, as well as other expenses paid to the employees, excluding the expenses paid to the retired and the wages paid to the construction in progress.

  1. Income tax, value-added tax, stamp tax, real estate tax, vehicle and ship tax, property tax

  2. Other cash flow related to operation

Investing activities

purchasing and selling investments

For example:

In 2000s, 莲花味精 invest in PPE for manufacturing aginomoto using wheat rather than corn.

Investment in R&D.

Investing activities-Inflow

(1) cash received from the sale, transfer or recovery of short-term investment and long-term equity investment other than cash equivalents, as well as the cash received from the recovery of long-term debt investment principal. It does not include non cash assets recovered.

(2) Cash received from investment return. It reflects the cash dividends and interests obtained by the enterprise due to equity investment and creditor’s rights investment, as well as the cash received from the profits distributed back from subsidiaries, associated enterprises and joint ventures, excluding stock dividends.

(3) Net cash received from disposal of fixed assets, intangible assets and other long-term assets.

This item reflects the net amount of cash obtained from selling fixed assets, intangible assets and other long-term assets (such as investment real estate) after deducting the relevant taxes and fees paid for the disposal of these assets. The cash received from the disposal of fixed assets, intangible assets and other long-term assets is close to the cash paid for disposal activities in time. The impact of disposal activities on cash flow can be better reflected by net amount. Because the amount is not large, it is reflected in net amount.

(4) Net cash received from disposal of subsidiaries and other operation units.The item is specially used to reflect the net amount of cash and cash equivalents held by the subsidiary and other business units on the disposal date and the related disposal expenses after the loss of control over subsidiaries and other business units (thus no longer the scope of consolidated statements of their receivers).

If the net amount is negative, it should be reflected in the item “other cash paid for investment activities”. For the net cash received from the disposal of subsidiaries and other business units, listed companies will generally disclose relevant information in the notes to the financial statements: “relevant information about acquiring or disposing subsidiaries and other business units in the current period”.

(5) other cash received from investment activities.

This item reflects other cash related to investment activities received by the enterprise in addition to the above items. If the value of other cash related to investment activities is relatively large, it should be reflected separately.

For other cash received related to investment activities, there are generally notes, and you can view the details.

Financing activities

Financing activities include obtaining or repaying capital, such as equity and long – term debt (shareholders vs creditors).

Cash inflows: cash receipts from issuing stock (common or preferred) or bonds and cash receipts from borrowing. (raising money from shareholder and debtholder, for example, ipo, seo, or loan contract)

Cash outflows: cash payments to repurchase stock (e.g., treasury stock), to pay dividends, and to repay bonds and other borrowings

Inflows

(1) cash received from investors’ investment. Reflect the cash received by the investor from the enterprise, including the actual net amount received from issuing shares, bonds and other means (the net amount of the issuing income minus the paid commission and other issuing expenses).

The audit, consultation and other expenses directly paid by the enterprise to raise funds by issuing stocks, bonds and other ways are reflected in the “other cash paid related to financing activities” project, not in this project.

(2) cash received from borrowing. It reflects the cash received by the enterprise from borrowing various short-term loans and long-term loans.

(3) Cash received from bond issuance.

This account reflects the net amount of money actually received by an enterprise to raise funds by issuing bonds (the net amount after the issuance income minus the commission paid and other issuance expenses).

It should be noted that the audit and consulting fees directly paid by the enterprise for raising funds by issuing bonds are not reflected in this project, but reflected in the item of “ other cash paying related to financing activities”.

(4) Other cash received related to financing activities.

This item reflects other cash related to financing activities received by the enterprise in addition to the above items. If the value of other cash related to financing activities is relatively large, it should be reflected separately. For other cash received related to financing activities, there are generally notes, and you can view the details.

Outflows

(1) cash paid for debt repayment. It reflects that the enterprise repays the

principal of the debt in cash, including the loan principal and bond

principal of the financial enterprise. The loan interest and bond interest

repaid by the enterprise shall be reflected in the item of “cash paid for

distribution of dividends, profits or repayment of interest”, and shall not

be included in this item.

(2) cash paid for distribution of dividends, profits or payment of interest.

Reflect the cash dividends actually paid by the enterprise, the profits paid to other investors and the interest paid on loans and bonds, etc.

(3) Other cash payments related to financing activities

In addition to the above items, the project reflects other cash paid by the enterprise related to financing activities, such as audit and consulting fees paid directly by the enterprise for raising funds by issuing stocks and bonds, cash paid for each period of financial leasing, and cash paid for the purchase and construction of fixed assets and intangible assets by instalments. For other cash payments related to financing activities, there are generally notes, and you can view the details.
