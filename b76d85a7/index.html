2.3HowDoFirmsManageFinancialRisk?

Created2023-11-20|Updated2024-04-28|FRM
|Word Count:1.4k|Reading Time:7mins

Risk Management Strategies

Make four basic choices in risk management ：

  1. Avoid or undertake risk

  2. Whether transfer risk to third parties

  3. Mitigate risk or not

  4. Assume or not assume risk.

Before making the choice, a risk appetite【风险偏好】 must be determined.

Risk Appetite

Risk appetite describes the amount and types of risk a firm is willing to accept. This is in contrast to risk capacity, which describes the maximum amount of risk a firm can absorb.

Risk appetite是公司所处的舒适的风险范围；

承担低于risk appetite的风险，公司会不满足于现有利益；

承担高于 risk appetite的风险，公司会难以承担。

profile是公司当前承担的风险水平；

capacity是公司所能承受的风险区间。

Issues related with risk appetite

  • The board【董事会】 must characterize【制定】 an appropriate “ risk appetite” for the firm.

  • Be connected to a firm’s overall business strategy and capital plan【经济资本计划】.

  • Clear communication throughout the firm of the firm’s risk appetite and risk position.

  • Effective risk management program should be consistent with fundamental strategic and risk appetite choices.

  • Risk appetites can be expressed in a number of ways, including quantitative and qualitative statements.

  • Banks have to choose their level of risk subject to external constraints【不能投资禁止性行业】

Risk Management Tools

Risk Limits【risk appetite 的定量表达】

Limits vary by the nature of the risk, the competitive positioning of the firm, and the span of its activities.

Practically speaking, these limits should be designed such that the probability of exceeding them during the normal situation of business is low.

Limit Nature Example Weakness
Stop Loss Limits【止损限额】 Loss threshold and associated action (e.g., close out, escalation) Will not prevent future exposure, only limit realized losses【限制已经发生的损失】.
Notional Limits【本金限制】 Notional size of exposure Notional amount may not be strongly related to economic risk of derivative instruments, especially options.
Risk Specific Limits【特别风险限制】 Limits referencing some special feature of risk in question (e.g., liquidity ratios for liquidity risk) These limits are difficult to aggregate; may require specialized knowledge to interpret.
Maturity【限制购买到期时间过长的资产】/Gap Limits Limit amount of transactions that mature or reset/ reprice in each time period These limits reduce the risk that a large volume of transactions will need to be dealt with in a given time frame, with all the operational and liquidity risks this can bring. But they do not speak directly to price risk.
Concentration Limits【集中度限制】 Limits of concentrations of various kinds (e.g., to individual counterparties, or product type) These limits must be set with the understanding of correlation risks. They may not capture correlation risks in stressed markets.
Greek Limits【描述衍生产品风险】 Option positions need to be limited in terms of their unique risk characteristics (e.g., delta, gamma, vega risk) These limits suffer from all the classic model risks and calculation may be compromised at trading desk level without the right controls and independence.
Value-at-Risk (VaR) Aggregate statistical number VaR suffers from all the classic model risks and may be misinterpreted by senior management. Specifically, VaR does not indicate how bad a loss might get in an unusually stressed market.
Stress,Sensitivity, and Scenario Analysis These limits are based on exploring how bad things could get in a plausible worst-case scenario. Stress tests look at specific stresses. Sensitivity tests look at the sensitivity of a position or portfolio to changes in key variables. Scenario modeling looks at given real-world scenarios (hypothetical or historical). Varies in sophistication. Dependent on deep knowledge of the firm’s exposures and market behavior. Difficult to be sure that all the bases are covered (e.g., there are endless possible scenarios).

“Realized losses” 表示已经实际发生的损失，即已经实现的损失。这种损失通常是在某次交易或投资中出售或减值的资产造成的，也可以是由于公司经营活动的不利结果而导致的损失。

与之相对的是 “unrealized losses”，即尚未实现的损失，它们表示的是当前市场价值低于购入成本的资产的账面价值下降，但这些资产尚未被出售或减值。

Derivatives

In many cases, the risk manager will decide to transfer a portion of a financial risk to the risk management markets.

转移风险

The use of these instruments requires firms to make key decisions based on their specific needs

forward:约定未来以某一价格购买某一产品的合约.即远期合约。

futures:forward的标准化。exchange-listed即交易所上市的。

swap：互换，是在一定期间内的一系列远期。

call option:call option的价格就是不履约的代价。需要支付期权费。获得的是不履约的权利。

exotic option：过于复杂的期权统称为exotic option，如shoot option。

swaption：swap+option。未来有权利进入swap。

Hedging Philosophy【对冲逻辑】

Hedging:套期保值，一般在对外贸易中应用，规避掉外汇风险。

Just because a risk can be hedged does not mean that it should be hedged.

Hedging is simply a tool and, like any tool, it has limitations.

  • Hedging has advantages.

  • Hedging has disadvantages.

  • How to hedge?

Advantages of Hedging Risk Exposures

  1. By employing risk management tools, management can better achieve the board’s objectives.【达成管理目标】

  2. Hedging reduces the cost of capital and enhances the ability to finance growth【融资能力】.

  3. Hedging increases the debt capacity of companies increasing interest tax deductions

  4. increasing interest tax deductions【利息递减税收】

  5. A firm can stabilize its cost through hedging

    1. A competitive advantage

  6. Purchasing insurance is expensive

Disadvantages of Hedging Risk Exposures

  1. Using hedging tools cannot increase the value of the firm.

    1. Franco Modigliani and Merton Miller (M&M), laid out in 1958: the value of a firm cannot be changed merely by means of financial transactions.

  2. Active hedge may distract management from its core business.【偏离主营业务】

  3. Hedging requires specific skills【特殊技巧】.

    1. A careless risk management strategy can drag a firm down even more quickly than the underlying risk.

  4. Risk management strategy has compliance cost【合规成本】, including disclosure【披露】.

  5. Hedging could increase the firm’s earnings variability due to the gap between accounting earnings【会计报表的收益】 and economic cash flows【实际的现金】.

economic cash flows更能体现企业的真实业绩。

  1. The problem will be amplified by poor communication.

How to hedge?

Static and Dynamic hedging strategies

  • The latter is more complex, time consuming and expensive

Hedge operational risk【运营风险】 and financial risk【金融风险】

  • Hedging operational risk means stabilizing expenses and revenues

就是去对冲 economic cash flow

  • Hedging financial risk means stabilizing assets and liabilities

稳定accounting earings

  • E.g. Price risk, interest rate risk and foreign exchange risk

A firm may misunderstand the type of risk that it faces. It may map or measure the risk incorrectly, fail to notice changes in the market structure, or suffer from a rogue traders on its team.
