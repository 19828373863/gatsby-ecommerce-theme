Short‐Term Finance and Planning

Balance Sheet Model of the Firm

How much short-term cash flow does a company need to pay its bills?

Tracing Cash and Net Working Capital

Current Assets are cash and other assets that are expected to be converted to cash within the year.

  • Cash and Cash equivalents: the most liquid current assets.

    • An investment normally counts as a cash equivalent when it has a short maturity period of 90 days or less.

  • Marketable Securities: the account where the total value of liquid investments that can be quickly converted to cash without reducing their market value is entered.

第1题 Short-term marketable securities generally have [单选题]
A. high maturity risk.
B. little, if any, marketability.
C. significant default risk.【重大的违约风险】
D. maturities between one and two years.
E. a high level of liquidity. (答案)

  • Accounts Receivable【应收账款】: the balance【余额】 of money due to a firm for goods or services delivered or used but not yet paid for by customers.

  • Inventory【存货】: goods available for sale and raw materials used to produce goods available for sale.

  • Prepaid Liabilities【预付账款】: advance payments made by a company for goods and services to be received in the future.

Current Liabilities are obligations that are expected to require cash payment within the year.

  • Accounts payable【应付账款】: amounts due to vendors or suppliers for goods orservices received that have not yet been paid for.

  • Short-term debt【短期负债】: such as bank loans or commercial paper issued to fund operations

  • Dividends payable【应付股利】

  • Notes payable【应付票据】

  • Current maturities of long-term debt

  • Interest payable【应付利息】 on outstanding debts, including long-term obligations

  • Income taxes owed within the next year【所得税】

Defining Cash in Terms of Other Elements

净营运资本NWC【net working capital】

=流动资产-流动负债

=（总资产-非流动资产FA）-（总资产-股东权益-非流动负债)

=(股东权益Equity+非流动负债LTD)–非流动资产FA

=长期资本-长期资产

The basic balance sheet identity can be written as:
$$NWC + FA = LTD + Equity$$

Net working capital is cash plus other current assets, less current liabilities:
$$NWC = Cash + Other current assets − CL $$

Rearrange terms:

$$ Cash = LTD+Equity+CL−Current assets other than cash−FA$$

Cash = LTD+Equity − NWC (excluding cash) −FA

An increase in long-term debt and or equity leads to an increase in cash—as does a decrease in fixed assets or a decrease in the non-cash components of net working capital.

Activities that increase cash are called sources of cash

Activities that decrease cash are called uses of cash

Example: Sources and Uses

If accounts payable【应付账款】 go up by $100, does this indicate a source or a use?

Accounts payable are what we owe our suppliers. This is a short-term debt.

If it rises by $100, we have effectively borrowed the money, which is a source of cash.

What if accounts receivable【应收账款】 go up by $100?

  • Receivables are what our customers owe to us

  • An increase of $100 in accounts receivable means that we have loaned the money;

  • this is a use of cash

从公式的角度很好理解。

The Operating Cycle and the Cash Cycle

The Operating Cycle【经营周期】 is the length of time it takes a company to acquire inventory, sell it, and collect for it. It has two distinct components:

  • Inventory period【存货期】: the time it takes the company to acquire and sell the inventory.
  • Accounts receivable period【应收账款期】: the time it takes the company to collect on the sale.

The Cash Cycle is the number of days that it takes for a company to convert its investments in inventory and other resources into cash flows from sales.【现金周期是指公司将其在库存和其他资源上的投资转化为销售现金流所需要的天数。】

$$Cash Cycle = Operating Cycle − Accounts Payable Period$$

Calculating the Operating and Cash Cycles

Start by gathering some balance sheet information

From the most recent income statement【利润表/损益表】

  • Net sales: $11,500

Net sales 指的是企业在一定时期内通过销售商品或提供服务所获得的 收入总额，扣除 销售退回、销售折扣 和 销售折让 等因素后的 净额。

  • Cost of goods sold: $8,200

Inventory period

为什么要用平均数？因为资产负债表反映的是某个时点数，而损益表反映的的期间数。

  • Average inventory = (2,000+3,000)/2 = 2,500

  • Inventory turnover = 8,200【销售成本】 / 2,500 = 3.28 times

  • Inventory period = 365 / 3.28 = 111.3 days

Receivables period

  • Average receivables = (1,600+2,000)/2 = 1,800

  • Receivables turnover【使用净销售收入】 = 11,500 / 1,800 = 6.39 times

  • Receivables period = 365 / 6.39 = 57.1 days

Operating cycle = 111.3 + 57.1 = 168.4 days

Payables Period

  • Average payables = (750+1,000)/2 = 875

  • Payables turnover【应付账款周转率】 = 8,200【注意分子是成本】 / 875 = 9.37 times

  • Payables period = 365 / 9.37 = 38.9 days

Cash Cycle = 168.4 - 38.9 = 129.5 days

We have to finance our inventory for 129.5 days.

If we want to reduce our financing needs, we need to look carefully at our receivables and inventory periods - they both seem excessive.【这意味着公司需要占用大量资金来维持库存，这增加了资金成本和风险。】

Some Aspects of Short-Term Financial Policy【短期财务政策的一些方面】

There are two elements of the policy that a firm adopts for short-term finance.【公司采用的短期融资政策包括两个要素。】

  • The size of the firm’s investment in current assets, usually measured relative to the firm’s level of total operating revenues.【公司在流动资产上的投资规模，通常相对于公司的总营业收入水平来衡量。】

    • Flexible
    • Restrictive

  • Alternative financing policies for current assets, usually measured as the proportion of short-term debt to long-term debt.【流动资产的替代融资政策，通常以短期债务与长期债务的比例来衡量。】

    • Flexible
    • Restrictive

Size of Investment in Current Assets【流动资产投资规模】

A flexible short-term finance policy would maintain a high ratio of current assets to sales.

灵活的短期融资政策将保持流动资产与销售额之间的高比率。

  • Keeping large cash balances and investments in marketable securities【保持大量现金余额和有价证券投资】

  • Large investments in inventory【对库存进行大量投资】

  • Liberal credit terms【宽松的信用条款】

核心就是流动资产多。

A restrictive short-term finance policy would maintain a low ratio of current assets to sales.【限制性的短期融资政策将保持流动资产与销售额之间的低比率。】

  • Keeping low cash balances, no investment in marketable securities【保持低现金余额，不投资有价证券】

  • Making small investments in inventory【对库存进行少量投资】

  • Allowing no credit sales (thus no accounts receivable)【不允许信用销售（因此没有应收账款）】

Carrying Costs【持有成本】 and Shortage Costs【短缺成本】

Managing current assets can be thought of as involving a trade-off between carrying costs and shortage costs.【管理流动资产可以被认为是涉及承载成本和短缺成本之间的权衡。】

Carrying Costs【持有成本】: Costs that rise with the level of investment in current assets

  • Opportunity cost【机会成本】

  • Maintenance Cost【维持成本】

Shortage Costs: Costs that fall with the level of investment in current assets

  • Trading, or order, costs: Order costs are the costs of placing an order for more cash (brokerage costs) or more inventory (production setup costs).【交易或订单成本：订单成本是指下订单的成本，例如对更多现金（经纪成本）或更多库存（生产设置费用）。】

  • Costs related to safety reserves: These are the costs of lost sales, lost customer goodwill, and disruption of production schedules【与安全储备相关的成本：这些是与销售损失、客户信誉损失以及生产计划中断相关的成本】

Alternative Financing Policies

A flexible short-term finance policy means a low proportion of short-term debt relative to long-term financing.【灵活的短期融资政策意味着短期债务相对于长期融资的比例较低。】

A restrictive short-term finance policy means a high proportion of short-term debt relative to long-term financing.【限制性的短期融资政策意味着短期债务相对于长期融资的比例很高。】

In an ideal world, short-term assets are always financed with short-term debt, and long-term assets are always financed with long-term debt.【在理想的世界里，短期资产总是用短期债务来融资，长期资产总是用长期债务来融资。】

  • In this world, net working capital is zero.【在这个世界里，净营运资本为零。】

Different Strategies for Financing Current Assets【为流动资产融资的不同策略】

Flexible Strategy: long-term financing covers more than the total asset requirement, even at seasonal peaks.【灵活策略：长期融资覆盖了超过总资产需求，即使在季节性高峰期也是如此。】

Restrictive Strategy: long-term financing does not cover the total asset requirement, the firm must borrow short term to make up the deficit【限制性策略：长期融资不覆盖总资产需求，公司必须通过短期借款来弥补差额】

Which is best?

There is no definitive answer.

The following considerations must be included

  • Cash reserves【现金储备】

  • Maturity hedging【久期对冲】

  • Term structure【期限结构】

Cash Budgeting【现金预算】

A cash budget is a primary tool of short-run financial planning.【现金预算是短期财务规划的主要工具。】

The idea is simple: Record the estimates of cash receipts【收入】 and disbursements【支出】.【其理念很简单：记录现金收入和支出的估计。】

Cash Receipts【现金收入】

  • Arise from sales, but we need to estimate when we actually collect【来自销售，但我们需要估计实际收款的时间】

Cash Outflow【现金流出】

  • Payments of Accounts Payable【应付账款的支付】

  • Wages, Taxes, and other Expenses【工资、税费和其他费用】

  • Capital Expenditures【资本支出】

  • Long-Term Financial Planning【长期财务规划】

Example

Pet Treats Inc. receives all income from sales【Pet Treats Inc. 从销售中获取所有收入】

Sales estimates (in millions)【销售估计（单位：百万）】

  • Q1 = 500; Q2 = 600; Q3 = 650; Q4 = 800; Q1 next year = 550

Accounts receivable

  • Beginning receivables【期初应收账款】= $250

  • Average collection period 【平均收款期】= 30 days

Accounts payable【应付账款】

  • Purchases = 50% of next quarter’s sales

  • Beginning payables【期初应付账款】 = 125

  • Accounts payable period【应付账款周期】 is 45 days

Other expenses

  • Wages, taxes and other expense are 30% of sales【工资、税费和其他费用占销售的30%】

  • Interest and dividend payments are $50【利息和股息支付为50美元】

  • A major capital expenditure of $200 is expected in the second quarter【预计第二季度将有200美元的主要资本支出】

The initial cash balance is $80 and the minimum balance is $50【初始现金余额为80美元，最低余额为50美元】

ACP = 30 days, this implies that 2/3 of sales are collected in the quarter made, and the remaining 1/3 are collected the following quarter.

Beginning receivables of $250 will be collected in the first quarter.

Payables period is 45 days, so half of the purchases will be paid for each quarter, and the remaining will be paid the following quarter.

Beginning payables = $125

The Short-Term Financial Plan【短期融资计划】

The most common way to finance a temporary cash deficit is to arrange a short-term loan.【最常见的解决临时现金短缺的方法是安排一笔短期贷款。】

Unsecured Loans【无抵押贷款】

  • Line of credit (at the bank)【信用额度（在银行）】

Secured Loans【抵押贷款】

  • Accounts receivable can be either assigned or factored.【应收账款可以“转让”或“保理”。】

  • Inventory loans use inventory as collateral【抵押】.

Other Sources

  • Banker’s acceptance【银行承兑汇票】

  • Commercial paper【商业票据】

Summary

This section introduced the management of short-term finance. Short-term finance involves short-lived assets and liabilities.

本节介绍了短期财务管理。短期融资涉及短期资产和负债。

Managing short-term cash flows involves the minimization of costs. The two major costs are carrying costs and shortage costs.

管理短期现金流涉及成本最小化。两大主要成本是持有成本和短缺成本。

In an ideal economy, a firm could perfectly predict its short-term uses and sources of cash and net working capital could be kept at zero.

在一个理想的经济环境中，企业可以完美预测其短期用途和现金来源，并且净营运资本可以保持在零。

The financial manager can use the cash budget to identify short-term financial needs. The cash budget tells the manager what borrowing is required or what lending will be possible in the short term.

财务经理可以使用现金预算来确定短期财务需求。现金预算告诉经理需要提供什么贷款，或者在短期内可能提供什么贷款。

The firm has a number of possible ways of acquiring funds to meet short-term shortfalls【短缺】, including unsecured and secured loans.

公司有多种可能的方式来获得资金以满足短期短缺，包括无担保和有担保贷款。
